# Adaptive Geometry Systems

Adaptive Geometry Systems explores how spatial geometry, LiDAR sensing, environmental conditions, and motion behavior can support future autonomy, mapping, and digital twin systems.

This project is built from a geomatics and airborne LiDAR perspective, focusing on how real-world geometry and sensor behavior influence movement through space.

---

## Core Ideas

- Geometry-aware motion planning
- Survey route geometry for vehicle trajectories
- Statistical learning of spatial behavior
- LiDAR confidence and sensor uncertainty
- Living maps and dynamic environmental intelligence
- Terrain, visibility, slope, and curvature-based risk

---

## Purpose

This repository is an idea archive and prototype lab. It is not meant to be a production autonomous driving system.

The goal is to explore concepts, build small working prototypes, and connect geomatics, surveying, airborne LiDAR, and spatial intelligence with autonomy and future mapping systems.

## Spatial Reference Framework

A key concept within Adaptive Geometry Systems is the use of transportation reference systems and measured roadway geometry as an additional source of environmental intelligence.

Traditional surveying and geomatics workflows rely on:

- Spatial control
- Linear Referencing Systems (LRS)
- Route measures
- Stationing
- Measured geometry
- Known reference frameworks

This project explores how these concepts may complement:

- LiDAR perception
- Localization
- Motion prediction
- Mapping systems
- Future autonomous navigation systems

Particular interest is placed on:

- LRS route measurements
- Route geometry
- Curvature
- Stationing concepts
- Spatial confidence metrics
- Geometry-derived risk indicators

## LiDAR and Sensor Confidence

Many autonomous systems focus heavily on object detection and classification.

This project also explores the concept of sensor confidence and environmental uncertainty.

Areas of interest include:

- LiDAR confidence measurements
- Environmental visibility
- Sensor uncertainty
- Terrain effects
- Occlusion awareness
- Dynamic environmental conditions

The goal is to better understand how confidence in spatial observations can influence motion behavior and decision-making.
